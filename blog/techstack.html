Essential Tech Stack for Course Creators

Don't build your course business on shaky ground. Here are the 7 tools you actually need (and the 15 you can ignore).

Kim Deepsyt

Course Launch Strategist

Table of Contents

The Problem 7-Tool Stack Tools to Ignore Day-by-Day Setup

You've seen the headlines: "Top 50 Tools Every Course Creator Needs!" or "The Ultimate List of 100+ SaaS Apps for Your Business". It's overwhelming and frankly, it's bullshit. Most of those lists are affiliate marketing disguised as advice.

After helping 300+ creators launch their courses, here's what I've learned: you need exactly 7 tools to build a six-figure course business. Everything else is just noise.

The Problem with "All-in-One" Platforms

Before we dive into the stack, let's address the elephant in the room. Why not just use Teachable, Thinkific, or Kajabi?

  • Vendor Lock-in: Once you're in, you're stuck. Migrating 1,000+ students is a nightmare.
  • Limited Customization: Want to add a simple quiz type? Good luck.
  • Revenue Share: That 10% transaction fee adds up fast. On a $10,000 launch, you're paying $1,000 just for processing.

The 7-Tool Tech Stack That Actually Works

1. Domain & DNS: Cloudflare ($0/mo)

Cloudflare's free plan gives you DNS management, SSL certificates, and basic CDN. It's the foundation everything else builds on.

Pro tip: Set up your domain through Cloudflare Registrar for wholesale pricing (no markup).

2. Website Builder: Webflow ($29/mo)

Webflow gives you the design flexibility of custom code with the ease of a visual builder. Perfect for landing pages, sales pages, and your main site.

Skip the CMS plan. You don't need dynamic content for a course site.

3. Course Platform: Podia ($39/mo)

Podia offers unlimited everything‚Äîcourses, students, uploads‚Äîfor one flat fee. No revenue sharing, no transaction fees.

Alternative: Teachable at $39/mo if you prefer their interface, but you'll pay 5% transaction fees.

4. Email Marketing: ConvertKit ($29/mo)

ConvertKit's visual automations make it easy to create complex email sequences without a marketing degree.

Start with the $29 plan. You can always upgrade when you hit 1,000 subscribers.

5. Payment Processing: Stripe (2.9% + 30¬¢)

Industry standard for online payments. Integrates with everything and handles all the compliance headaches.

Set up webhooks for instant course access after purchase.

6. Analytics: Fathom Analytics ($14/mo)

Privacy-first analytics that don't slow down your site. See exactly where your sales are coming from without compromising user privacy.

Google Analytics is free but will kill your page speed scores.

7. Community: Circle ($49/mo)

Circle combines the engagement of Facebook Groups with the control of your own platform. No algorithm, no distractions.

Skip this initially. Start with a free Facebook Group and upgrade when you have 100+ students.

Total Monthly Cost: $160

Compare that to Kajabi's $199/mo plan (which still charges 0% transaction fees), and you're saving $39/mo while getting more flexibility.

The 15 Tools You Can Ignore (At Least Initially)

  • Webinar platforms (Zoom works fine)
  • Affiliate software (manual tracking works for 20-50 affiliates)
  • Survey tools (Google Forms is free)
  • Calendar booking (Calendly's free tier is enough)
  • Social media schedulers (manual posting is more authentic)
  • SEO tools (Google Search Console is all you need)
  • Heatmap software (overkill for most course sites)
  • Membership plugins (Podia handles this)
  • Video hosting (Vimeo/YouTube work fine)
  • Design tools (Canva's free tier is sufficient)
  • CRM systems (ConvertKit handles basic CRM)
  • Help desk software (email works for < 500 students)
  • Quiz builders (Podia has basic quizzes)
  • Certificate generators (manual PDFs work)
  • Mobile app builders (your students won't use them)

Setting Up Your Stack: Day-by-Day Plan

Here's exactly how to set everything up in one weekend:

Day 1: Foundation (2 hours)

  • Buy domain through Cloudflare
  • Set up DNS records
  • Create Webflow account and connect domain
  • Basic Webflow template setup

Day 2: Course & Email (3 hours)

  • Sign up for Podia and create your first course
  • Set up ConvertKit and create opt-in form
  • Connect Stripe to Podia
  • Install Fathom Analytics tracking

Day 3: Test & Launch (1 hour)

  • Test purchase flow end-to-end
  • Send test email sequence
  • Check analytics tracking
  • Soft launch to 5 beta users

When to Upgrade Your Stack

Don't upgrade until you hit these milestones:

  • Circle ($49/mo): When you have 100+ students and daily questions
  • Higher ConvertKit plan ($50/mo): When you hit 1,000 subscribers
  • Zapier ($19.99/mo): When you need to connect 3+ tools
  • Webflow CMS ($23/mo): When you want dynamic blog content
  • First employee: When you're making $10k+/month consistently

Remember: your tech stack won't make or break your course business. Your content, marketing, and student results will. Start simple, validate demand, then optimize.
