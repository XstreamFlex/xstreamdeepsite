Price Your Course Right the First Time

Most creators underprice their courses by 50-70%. Learn the psychology behind pricing and the exact formula to maximize revenue without losing customers.

Kim Deepsyt

Course Launch Strategist

Table of Contents

The Psychology of Pricing The 3-Part Formula Pricing Tactics That Work Common Mistakes Real Case Study

When Sarah came to me with her $97 course, I asked her one question: "How did you come up with that price?" Her answer was the same one I hear 90% of the time: "It felt right."

Here's the thing: your gut feeling is probably costing you thousands. After analyzing 500+ course launches, I've discovered that most creators underprice by 50-70% because they don't understand the psychology behind value perception.

The Psychology of Pricing

Price isn't just a number—it's a signal of value, quality, and transformation. When someone sees a $97 course next to a $997 course, they automatically assume the higher-priced option delivers 10x the results.

Key Insight

Students don't buy courses—they buy transformations. Price your course based on the value of the outcome, not the hours of content you provide.

The 3-Part Pricing Formula

After testing dozens of pricing models, here's the formula that consistently maximizes revenue:

  1. Calculate the transformation value - What's the outcome worth to your student?
  2. Determine your authority score - New creators start at 0.3, proven experts at 1.0
  3. Apply the formula: Price = (Transformation Value × 0.1) × Authority Score

Example: If your course helps someone land a $60k job, and you're a new creator: ($60,000 × 0.1) × 0.3 = $1,800.

Pricing Tactics That Work

1. The Anchor Effect

Always present your premium option first. When students see a $2,000 option first, your $497 course feels like a bargain.

2. Payment Plans That Convert

Offer 3-6 month payment plans. They increase conversion by 23% while maintaining your full price.

3. The Decoy Effect

Create a middle tier that's intentionally less attractive. Most students will choose your premium option.

Common Pricing Mistakes

  • Underpricing to get students: Signals low quality and attracts bargain hunters
  • Over-discounting: Never offer more than 20% off your regular price
  • One-size-fits-all pricing: Always offer at least two tiers
  • Ignoring payment processing fees: Factor 3-5% into your price

Real Case Study: From $97 to $2,497

Meet David, a coding bootcamp graduate who created a Python course. Initially priced at $97, he had 150 students but was barely covering his costs.

The Transformation

After working together, we repositioned his course as a career transformation program:

  • New price: $2,497 with payment plans
  • Added career coaching component
  • Created job placement partnerships
  • Updated messaging to focus on salary outcomes

Result: 40 students at $2,497 = $99,880 (vs. $14,550 previously).

Your Next Steps

Don't wait for the "perfect" price. Start with the formula, test with a small group, and adjust based on feedback.
